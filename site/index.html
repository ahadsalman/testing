Bhutto had issues with NAP after he came into power, in early 1972 , Bhutto had called out NAP and designated them as unfaithful. NAP had a coalition with Jamat-e-Islami and other small political parties in 1972 but were displaced in 1973. Bhutto had no support of Balochistan and NWFP at that time. Later, in 1975 Bhutto’s lieutenant Hayat Mohammed Khan Sherpao was assassinated in a bomb at Peshawar university. Bhutto had gone for a tour in another country and he rushed home after this news and invoked the political parties act of 1962 and dissolved NAP and its allies.

A trade agreement was signed between Pakistan and the government of India, the key points of this agreement was that the two nations should follow the stated laws and regulations in their countries, take all necessary actions to ease, reinforce and diversify trade between the two countries. It was designed for the betterment of the relations of the two countries, helping and assisting each other getting more business.
1976 The year 1976 was a significant one in the history of Pakistan. It was the year when people's act, which was passed by Prime Minister Zulfikar Ali Bhutto, came into effect. This act allowed the public to have a say in the politics of their country. Nawaz shareef enters politics after the nationalism that was imposed by Bhutto. Representation of people’s act . The people's act 1976 was passed by the national assembly and made the fundamental principles of democracy applicable to all citizens of Pakistan irrespective of their religion, caste, creed or sex. This act also made it illegal for any government body to discriminate against any citizen on the basis of religion, caste, creed or sex. Political events in Pakistan in 1976: 1977 The constitution of Pakistan (1973) was suspended by General Zia ul Haq on July 5, 1977. In 1977, the military coup in Pakistan led by General Zia-ul-Haq overthrew Prime Minister Zulfikar Ali Bhutto. The general elections were held in 1977 with the support of the martial law government The coup was masterminded by Chief of Army Staff General Gul Hassan Khan and executed by the Pakistani Armed Forces. The military government that followed enforced martial law, censored the press and imposed other restrictions on civil liberties - The constitution of Pakistan (1973) was suspended by General Zia ul Haq on July 5, 1977 - The general elections were held in 1977 with the support of the martial law government In addition to this, 1977 also saw some very important political events take place. The most significant of these events was the coup d'état led by General Muhammad Zia-ul-Haq on July 5th 1977. As a result of this coup d'état, General Muhammad Zia-ul-Haq became president and Chief Martial Law Administrator of Pakistan for life. The Pakistani public had been very vocal about their discontent with Bhutto's rule and were happy to see him go
